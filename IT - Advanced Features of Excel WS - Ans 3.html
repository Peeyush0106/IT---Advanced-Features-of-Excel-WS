Microsoft Excel Chart and its Types


What is a chart? Click on the this text to get the answer. A chart is a graphical representation of data that shows the values in pictorial and in an attractive manner which makes it easier for the users to search for any information from the graph.

Here is the explanation for the different components of a chart in Microsoft Excel.
Note: Click on any component box to see the image of that component.

Chart Title

  1. It is the main idea/theme of the chart.
  2. It tells what the chart is about.

Chart Area

  1. It is the area where all the components of a chart reside.
  2. It is the main area or the carrier or the chart.

Plot Area

  1. It is the area where the plotting of the chart occurs
  2. This does not include components like Legend, axises, etc.

Category Axis

  1. It is the horizontal axis.
  2. Also known as x-axis

Value Axis

  1. It is the vertical axis.
  2. Also known as y-axis

Gridlines

  1. These are the lines inside the plot area.
  2. They make the values of each category easier to read
  3. They are spaced equally
  4. They run from left to right and top to bottom

Category Name

  1. It is the main idea/theme of an axis.
  2. It tells what the axis is about.

Legend

  1. These are special colours, patterns, symbols.
  2. These special designs depict a particular category

Data Series

  1. These are the plotted bars or slices that show the value of a category.
  2. They are usually designed according to the designs of the legend.
